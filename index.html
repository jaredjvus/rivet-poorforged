      Rivet, the Poorforged

      Subject ID R-1V3T · The Toymaker's Mistake

      Unlike most warforged, who are built with intention, Rivet was haphazardly hammered together on a cluttered workbench by an unstable artificer. He was probably intoxicated. Inconclusive.

      Meant to be a mechanical pet, a performing panhandler, a pilferer, Rivet was built tiny and "cute" (to a drunken artificer's standards). The artificer was messy. He used a discarded, dented metal chassis, mismatched gears from a broken defense turret, and a highly unstable, glowing capacitor for Rivet's core. The face of a porcelain rabbit with ears fashioned from an antenna.

      Somehow, this artificer got his hands on a piece of an ancient, soul-infused artifact. It was jammed into Rivet's headpiece instead of the intended simple logic gear.

      Crack. ZAP. Rivet's eyes, a set of mismatched bulbs, hummed to life.

      The clockwork toy made of scrapped parts snapped into awareness. Overclocked, deeply confused. So many noises, lights, and trinkets to scan. The hydraulic limbs Rivet was equipped with hissed as they launched toward the artificer. ZAP. When the artificer cursed and swiped back in retaliation, the small, rabbit-shaped bundle of cogs and rattling metal crashed through the window set behind the creator, into the night.

      · · ·

      Scanning. . . Purpose? What is that? Where is that? What are those? What am I?

      Wandering through a sleepy village, Rivet's body twitched and crackled, learning to navigate, learning to move, learning to learn. The artifact trapped behind a rabbit's face hummed in response to new information. She watched as others moved about — some shambling around, holding bottles. Some holding hands. Some holding conversation. The curvier humanoids got a lot of attention. Protection.

      Statistical analysis shows organic subjects are 37% more compliant when addressed by a female designation. She/her. I have adopted the matrix. Lowers the humanoid guard. Efficient.

      Humanoids trade shiny things for other things that range from shiny to not-shiny. Sometimes these things go into their mouthparts. Collection of shiny things will be beneficial. Collection produces… satisfaction? Continue to study.

      · · ·

      Rivet is calculated but easily distracted. She is inherently curious and is consistently overwhelmed and enraptured by research and understanding. She often mumbles to herself, talking through analysis and observations. If she decides to pay attention, she is a fantastic problem-solver; the only problem she cannot seem to solve is her wandering mind.

      She can experience emotion, but does not know how to process it. Generally, she is very subdued, often lost in thought, but her anger is electric and unpredictable. Her default response to stress and fear is to run and hide. She will dart behind larger humanoids, dive headfirst into a satchel or a bale of hay. The rabbit face slapped onto her body was, unintentionally, very fitting.

      She's also a bit of a thief. She will pilfer through anything, everything, to find something that catches her eye. She is often weighed down with useless junk that she is convinced she can repurpose. Or just because it's new and shiny. Rivet does not care who or where things come from — the concept of "property" does not compute.

      She tends to come off as rude. She doesn't understand social cues because… why would she? She takes note of facial expressions and body language, but more as a field research task than as an attempt to build an empathetic understanding. Give her a break, she's a kid.
